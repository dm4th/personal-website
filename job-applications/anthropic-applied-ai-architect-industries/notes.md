## Remaining Gaps: How to Address in Conversation

**1. Advisory has been internal-GTM, not external vendor-representing.**
The motion is identical: run discovery, design architecture, build the trusted relationship, move the customer to adoption. The venue is different. I have been advising enterprise buyers on behalf of my own company rather than on behalf of Anthropic. That is a distinction worth acknowledging directly. My answer is that the skillset is the same and the Anthropic context actually helps: I would be advising on Claude, a platform I have built production systems on, rather than on a product I inherited from someone else.

**2. Vertical concentration in healthcare.**
Healthcare is the hardest enterprise vertical, which means the patterns transfer. If I can navigate HIPAA, BAA review cycles, and multi-site Epic integration conversations, I can navigate financial services compliance frameworks and manufacturing operational technology reviews. The domain specifics differ but the advisory structure is the same: find the real blocker, design around it, give the right stakeholder a concrete answer they can take to their approval chain. Worth acknowledging the concentration and framing the healthcare depth as signal quality rather than limitation.

**3. No direct "representing Anthropic to enterprise" track record.**
I have not been a vendor-side advisor for a foundation model company. I have been a buyer-side advisor helping enterprise customers evaluate and adopt AI. That perspective is actually useful: I know what enterprise buyers are scared of, what they ask in security reviews, and what the real adoption blockers are, because I have been on the other side of those conversations. That framing reframes the gap as an asset.

## Questions to Ask in the Interview

1. **What does the feedback loop between Applied AI Architects in the field and Anthropic's product team look like?** I have built systems to close this loop (the event-driven standup agent at Smarter Technologies). I want to understand whether Anthropic has a structured mechanism for field observations to influence the Claude roadmap, or whether that is something this team is still building.

2. **How does the role navigate the tension between moving quickly enough to be useful to a customer and the governance overhead enterprise IT approval chains require?** In my experience the biggest variable is how much the Applied AI Architect can do before IT security needs to be in the room. I want to understand where Anthropic draws that line and what infrastructure exists to accelerate the approval process.

3. **What does success look like in the first 90 days?** Specifically: is the expectation that an Applied AI Architect is running independent customer workshops in the first quarter, or is there a ramp that involves shadowing existing engagements first? I want to calibrate whether the onboarding model assumes domain expertise or builds it.

4. **How do you think about vertical depth vs. breadth on this team?** Is the expectation that each Applied AI Architect develops a primary vertical and a secondary vertical, or is the model generalist with a rotating cast of industry specialists brought in? I ask because I have deep healthcare depth and I want to understand whether that is an asset to specialize on or a starting point to expand from.

5. **What is the biggest unsolved problem in enterprise Claude adoption right now?** Not the product gaps (I can read the roadmap), but the adoption architecture gaps: the things that are technically possible today but that enterprise customers are not yet able to use in production because of governance, trust, or organizational readiness blockers. That is where I would want to focus first.
