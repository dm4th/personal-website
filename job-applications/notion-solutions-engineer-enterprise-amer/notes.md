I've run discovery, scoped implementations, engineered demos, and managed stakeholder alignment from CIO to IT security across 61 deals. The product changes; the process doesn't. And I'm not starting from zero on Notion — I've been building on the platform for two years and can demo and prototype natively from day one.

**"Salesforce experience is limited."**
Be upfront: I've used Salesforce as a reporting layer and data sync target, not as an admin or workflow builder. Then: I built the Notion API integration platform from scratch in under a month while managing 61 live deals simultaneously. Salesforce's data model and API are well-documented — I'm confident I'd cover the learning curve quickly and wouldn't be blocked on it in the field.

**"Your most recent titles (Director SE, VP Solutions) are senior to a standard IC SE role."**
Those titles reflected startup scope, not big-company org chart level. At a 15-30 person company, Director of SE means you are the SE org. What I'm actually looking for is the right SE motion at a company with a product I believe in. Notion is that company. I'm not stepping down — I'm stepping into the role I want to do well.

## Questions to ask in the interview

1. **"What does the enterprise SE motion look like end-to-end at Notion — where does the SE get involved, and where does hand-off to implementation or CSM typically happen?"** (Calibrates the scope of the role and where you'd spend the most time.)

2. **"How technical do the enterprise buyers tend to be — are you mostly working with IT and engineering, or primarily with ops and GTM leadership?"** (Helps you understand how to pitch your technical depth vs. business translation skills.)

3. **"How does the SE team approach demo engineering — is there shared infrastructure for building prospect-specific demos, or does each SE build their own?"** (Signals your platform-builder instinct and lets you connect your demo engineering background.)

4. **"What's the biggest objection enterprise accounts raise during evaluation — and what's the most effective thing an SE can do to address it?"** (Forces specificity about the real deal friction, gives you signal about what the role actually requires.)

5. **"What would a great first 90 days look like for a new enterprise SE — what would you want them to have done, and what would make you say 'this person gets it'?"** (Lets you align your ramp plan to their actual expectations, signals you're thinking about impact not just onboarding.)
