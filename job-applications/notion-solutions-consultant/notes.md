## Remaining gaps and how to address them

**"You haven't implemented Notion for an external client in a formal consulting capacity."**
Be direct: all my Notion depth is internal — as an admin, a builder, and a power user. But the distinction between "internal admin" and "external consultant" underestimates what internal admin work actually requires. I built a workspace from the ground up, governed its structure, integrated it into a production platform the whole company depended on, and scaled it to 76 prospect environments. That's architecture and governance thinking. The only thing that changes for a customer engagement is who I'm building it for.

**"Your enterprise buyer experience is concentrated in healthcare."**
Acknowledge it, then flip it. The problems Notion solves — fragmented workflows, tribal knowledge, unclear project ownership — exist in every industry. Healthcare is arguably the most organizationally complex environment to navigate. If I can align a CIO, a CFO, and an RCM operations director on an AI adoption decision, I can align a VP of Ops and a Head of Revenue on a Notion rollout. The consultative skill transfers; the domain shifts.

**"Your most recent titles are more senior than a Solutions Consultant IC role."**
Director of SE and VP of Solutions were startup-scale titles at companies with 15-30 people. They reflect scope, not org chart level. What I'm looking for is the right consultative motion at the right company — a product I believe in, enterprise deal complexity, and a team building something important. Notion is that company. I'm not here because I've run out of options at the senior level.

## Questions to ask in the interview

1. **"What does the ramp period look like for a new SC — how long before you're carrying a full book of enterprise accounts, and what does support look like during that period?"** (Tests whether onboarding is structured or sink-or-swim, signals you're thinking about ramp seriously.)

2. **"How does the SC motion differ across segments — what's different about the discovery and scoping process for a 5,000-person enterprise vs. a 500-person mid-market account?"** (Shows you understand deal nuance and want to be calibrated on where you'll actually spend time.)

3. **"Where are enterprise customers in their Notion maturity — are most deals greenfield adoption, consolidation from another tool, or expansion of an existing footprint?"** (Critical for understanding how to frame the discovery motion and what objections dominate.)

4. **"What does the feedback loop between SCs and product look like — how does enterprise customer feedback get back to the team building the product?"** (Shows you're thinking about the full SC motion, not just closing deals.)

5. **"What's the one thing the best SC on the team does that most candidates underestimate going in?"** (Open-ended, forces the interviewer to tell you what actually matters, gives you a chance to connect your experience to the answer.)
