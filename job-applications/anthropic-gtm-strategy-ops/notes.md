## Strongest Talking Points

**1. "I've been running RevOps without the title."**
At Smarter Technologies, the RevOps function was thin on paper and nonexistent in practice. I ran both SE and RevOps: pricing models for $250K to $40M deals, multi-year discount scenarios, Salesforce reporting for VP and CEO reviews, ICP scoring across 99 deals, pipeline health analytics used in executive reviews. Two people covered 62% of company pipeline through the system I built. The work looks identical to what a dedicated RevOps lead would produce. The org chart just looked different.

**2. "The FanDuel model is the consumption-based analytics playbook."**
Daily fantasy sports is structurally identical to API token consumption: users pay per action, high-intensity users generate disproportionate revenue, and retention within spending cohorts determines the business outcome. The Markov Chain Monte Carlo simulation I built modeled user-state transitions across 17 segments and reduced NFL season forecast error from 6-8% to within 2%. That is the same analytical framework Anthropic needs for usage-based pricing analytics. The variables differ; the structure is identical.

**3. "I've been on the other side of enterprise AI adoption."**
Two years of sitting across from the CISOs, CFOs, and clinical ops leaders evaluating AI in healthcare. I know what moves them from skeptical to convinced, what reporting they need to trust the numbers, and what the GTM motion looks like when the analytical infrastructure exists versus when it doesn't. That field context is something a pure ops hire doesn't bring.

**4. "I built the north star metric that changed two teams' roadmaps."**
At Action Network, the "Value Clicks" metric did not exist when I joined. I built it from behavioral analytics, pitched it to content and product leadership, and watched both teams change their roadmap priorities based on a number that had not existed three months earlier. That is the cross-functional alignment through analytical infrastructure that GTM Ops should create.

**5. "BigQuery at 500M users was my day job at Google."**
The GoogleSQL I used daily at Google is BigQuery's query language. I built P&L reporting suites, executive dashboards, and revenue forecasting models against BigQuery at a scale most candidates have never touched. It is a tool I have used at production scale, not something I have read documentation for.
