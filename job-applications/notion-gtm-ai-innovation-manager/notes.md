## Remaining gaps and how to address them

**"Your GTM AI work has been internal and self-directed, not a product launch at Notion's scale."**
Acknowledge it: building tools for your own team of 30 is different from shipping a GTM AI program to hundreds of field reps at a company with millions of users. Then: the design instincts — what sellers actually need, how to reduce friction in adoption, what instrumentation tells you about real usage — come from having been the seller and the builder simultaneously. Starting from inside the commercial org produces better-fitting tools than starting from a product org and working outward. The scaling is a new problem; the instincts for what to build are already trained.

**"Salesforce and HubSpot depth is limited."**
Listed as a nice-to-have, not a requirement. I built bidirectional Salesforce sync into my Notion platform — so the API and data model are familiar. I haven't been a Salesforce admin or workflow builder at depth, and I'd be upfront about that. The platform patterns are rampable; the harder thing to ramp is knowing what GTM teams actually need from an AI system, which I already know from having run one.

**"No formal GTM strategy or product commercialization background."**
This is a real gap if the role leans toward product marketing or launch sequencing. The JD reads more like a builder-operator role than a product marketer role — "hands-on building," "ship continuously," "work alongside engineering." That framing plays to the builder side of the background. If the interview surfaces a heavier strategy/launch angle, be honest about where the experience lives and connect to the operator credibility.

## Questions to ask in the interview

1. **"What does the current GTM AI stack look like at Notion — are there existing tools and workflows that need to be integrated, or is this closer to a greenfield build?"** (Critical for understanding scope and whether the role is architect-from-scratch vs. inherit-and-extend.)

2. **"How does this role sit relative to Revenue Operations and Data Science — who owns what, and where does the GTM AI Innovation Manager have full ownership vs. stakeholder influence?"** (Clarifies the actual authority and build vs. coordinate ratio of the role.)

3. **"What does success look like at 90 days and at one year — is there a specific workflow or productivity metric the team is hoping to move first?"** (Grounds the conversation in concrete deliverables and lets you connect to your instrumentation and ROI measurement experience.)

4. **"What has adoption looked like for AI tools the GTM team has tried before — where has friction been highest, and what drove or killed usage?"** (Shows you know that building the thing and getting people to use it are different problems, and signals you've thought about change management.)

5. **"How closely does this role work with Notion's product team on AI features — is there a feedback loop from the internal GTM tooling back into the roadmap?"** (Reveals whether there's a strategic product influence dimension to the role, and lets you talk about the feedback loops you built at Smarter Technologies.)
