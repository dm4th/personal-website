# Key Courses

### Operating Systems

The most practically useful course in the program. Multiprocessing and threading were the topics I found most compelling: understanding how the OS schedules work, manages memory, and handles concurrency at the C and assembly level. Everything I learned here shows up in how I think about system design.

Built in: C, Python, assembly language

### Data Structures and Algorithms

This course gave me the foundational vocabulary for thinking about computation: Big O analysis, space/time tradeoffs, graph traversal, sorting algorithms, and the various tree and hash structures that underlie most software. I've returned to these concepts more often than I expected.

Built in: Python, advanced data structures

### Digital Systems and Circuits

Circuit design turned out to be immensely satisfying: it felt like mathematical puzzle-solving with precise, verifiable answers. This course covered signal processing at the boundary between analog and digital: taking AC input signals, converting them through FPGA logic to digital outputs, and programming the processing logic in Verilog. The precision required pushed my ability to design complex systems.

Built in: Verilog, FPGA development, binary and assembly language

### Entrepreneurial Engineering

A startup pitch competition with a cross-disciplinary team of engineers and business students. I was the sole technical contributor. The experience was more valuable than I appreciated at the time. Working across functional boundaries, translating technical capabilities into business language, and learning that a good pitch requires both a compelling idea and a credible execution plan were lessons that stuck.

### Senior Capstone Project

A year-long collaboration with the electrical department to build MAGE, a mobile gaming console consisting of an Android app and four wireless hardware nodes, each with a gyroscope, button, and colored lights. We built two games: a Bop-It variant and a Capture the Nodes game where teams competed to control nodes.

I served as project manager, coordinating a team of talented engineers toward a common deliverable. Honest reflection: I wasn't the most knowledgeable person on the team for the hardware work. But I learned how to contribute meaningfully to a group that has skills you don't, which turned out to be one of the more durable lessons of college.

Built in: Android development, Bluetooth mesh networking, web development
