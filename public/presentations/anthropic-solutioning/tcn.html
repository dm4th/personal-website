Customer Challenge

Eligibility checks
before every visit

A PE-backed multi-state outpatient behavioral health network. Every appointment required a fresh E&B verification: active coverage, deductible status, copay amounts. No automation existed. The workflow was entirely manual, payer portal by portal, patient by patient.

Situation ~1,500 clinicians. ~300,000 eligibility lookups per month. Staff verified coverage manually before each session: 7 minutes per patient, a different portal for every carrier. Coverage status determined whether the appointment happened at all. A failed check meant a missed session and lost revenue.
Landscape Payer-by-payer feasibility analysis to find where 80% of appointment volume lived. Dominant payer covered ~38%, enabling a phased rollout that targeted the highest ROI carriers first.
Constraint Honest engineering estimate: 6 months. Sales committed 4. The 2-month gap became the first architectural constraint we designed around. Predictability under a fixed deadline matters more than probabilistic flexibility.

Appendix B

Observability
Architecture

Built in a 2-week recovery sprint after an undetected write error affected appointment records. The pre/post diff that found the scope of the problem became a permanent product capability.

Pre-run snapshot Before each overnight run: capture appointment count, coverage flags, and deductible fields for all patients in scope. Stored as a point-in-time baseline in the monitoring layer.
Post-run diff After the run: compare PMS state against the baseline. Flag any unexpected deletions or overwrites. Alert threshold: any appointment record modified outside the expected E&B fields.
Recovery value The layer identified the scope of the write error within hours, enabling targeted reconciliation. ~$45K in revenue recovered via the diff-identified records. Now runs on every overnight job as a standard health check.
