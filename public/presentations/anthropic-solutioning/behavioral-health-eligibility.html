Customer Challenge

Eligibility checks
before every visit

A PE-backed multi-state outpatient behavioral health network. Every appointment required a fresh E&B verification: not generic medical benefits, but mental-health and telehealth specific coverage that every payer encodes differently: visit limits, telehealth parity, behavioral carve-outs, copay, deductible. No automation existed. The workflow was entirely manual, payer portal by portal, patient by patient.

~300K
Lookups per month
Today's agent-run volume across ~1,500 clinicians; the champion's own April figure.
80%
Of volume in scope
Coverage target set by the feasibility model: which carriers, in what order.
4 mo
Given, vs 9 needed
Sales committed 4 months against a 9-month engineering instinct.
Situation Staff verified coverage manually before each session: ~7 minutes per check, a different portal for every carrier. The cost surfaced in three buckets: eligibility-related denial write-offs, copays missed at time of service, and verifier hours that scaled with volume; Medicaid churn added a first-of-month sweep of every upcoming patient.
Landscape Feasibility modeled from claims counts: payers map many-to-one onto portals, and the cumulative curve crossed 80% at seven portals, with Availity alone at ~38%. That curve was the rollout plan.
Constraint Engineering instinct: 9 months. Negotiated to 6. Sales committed 4, and the deal's lead seller was our CEO: not a line I could hold from below. That compression became the first architectural constraint we designed around. Predictability under a fixed deadline matters more than probabilistic flexibility.

Where Is the AI?

Four surfaces, each sized honestly. The judgment is knowing where the model earns its place.

Deterministic where correctness is sacred
AI where variation defeats rules  ·  human-gated until proven in production  ·  never oversold: the build's value is the system, the AI is placed surgically inside it
Foundational · Build-Time
Code Generation
LLMs wrote much of the deterministic business logic, working from a Master Automation Plan: every workflow specified as pseudo-code, with exception hierarchies and real payer-response samples. Context engineering before the tooling existed; this build predates Claude Code, so the spec had to be exact.
Shipped to Prod · ~2pt Lift
LLM Parsing, Long Tail
Net-new payer responses parsed from raw HTML into copay / coinsurance / deductible / OOP. Raised human-in-the-loop with pre-filled fields + screenshot, then graduated to auto-accept in production.
Smallest Slice
Voice Agents
Phone-based E&B checks for the long-tail payers with no usable portal at all. A small share of volume, and the honest answer for the last mile of coverage.
Recovery-Born
Building the Exception Analytics
The observability + exception-analytics product was scaffolded AI-assisted inside the 2-week recovery sprint: from incident response to an embedded, customer-facing product in weeks.

Appendix D

Observability
Architecture

Built in a 2-week recovery sprint after an undetected write error affected appointment records. The pre/post diff that found the scope of the problem became a permanent product capability.

Pre-Run Snapshot
Before each overnight run: capture appointment count, coverage flags, and deductible fields for all patients in scope. Stored as a point-in-time baseline.
Post-Run Diff
Compare PMS state against the baseline. Flag any unexpected deletion or overwrite: any appointment record modified outside the expected E&B fields alerts.
Known-Answer Re-Runs
The marginal cost of a check was ~$0, so patients with verified-correct benefits were re-run continuously: a regression suite of real patients proving the product still worked, catching silent drift before customers did.
~$45K
Revenue recovered
The diff found the write error's scope within hours, enabling targeted reconciliation of the affected records.
Every overnight run standard health check  ·  alerts on any unexpected write  ·  graduated from crisis tool to permanent product

Built in the 2-week recovery sprint; the same instrumentation seeded the analytics product in Appendix E.
