Customer Challenge

The bottleneck
moved downstream

A multi-state health system running Epic, ~$1-2B net patient revenue, mid-way through a two-year revenue-cycle transformation. We already had a clinical-AI deployment live there: this was a proven partnership, not a cold sale.

Situation Clinical AI was generating denial-appeal letters faster than staff could file them. The bottleneck had moved downstream to manual portal submission: 8-10 minutes per appeal, 312 appeals a month.
Volume 312 appeals/month requiring manual payer-portal login, form completion, PDF upload, and confirmation capture back into Epic. Parallel prior-auth team: 500-600 checks/day against a 4,000-account backlog.
Constraint Direct Epic write-back required write permissions: the single largest IT gate. The RCM leader said it plainly: "I don't trust easily." That sentence became the real specification.

Appendix B

Commercial
model detail

Transaction pricing aligns incentives: we get paid per successful automated submission. The appeals SOW is live and generating revenue today. Prior-auth is the next phase and the primary driver of the ~$1M/yr projection.

Pricing unit $2.50 per automated submission (appeals and prior-auth checks). $0.50 per follow-up action (status polling, resubmission). No seat licenses, no minimums: pure transaction model.
Live SOW today 312 appeals/month automated at $2.50 each. Full prior-auth backlog: 4,000 accounts, 500-600 checks/day. At 85-90% automation rate, that is ~450-540 automated checks daily.
Prior-auth at scale 12,500 checks/month in evaluation scope. At $2.50 and 87% automation: 10,875 automated checks/month. Annual run rate on prior-auth alone: ~$326K/yr. Combined with appeals and follow-up actions: path to $1M/yr includes expansion to additional sites and service lines.
Strategic value Transaction pricing with a customer already at $20M+ net patient lift from prior clinical-AI. Each new SOW extends the partnership footprint rather than requiring a fresh trust cycle.
